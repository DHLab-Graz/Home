Christopher Pollin, MA MA

Holds a Master degree in History and a Joint Master’s Degree in Digital Heritage. Currently he is a PhD candidate in Digital Humanities. Since 2016 he has been a research assistant at the Centre for Information Modelling (ZIM). His work includes technical development and data modelling in various DH projects. The main focus of his work is on semantic web technologies, resource discovery, and web programming. In addition to teaching in these fields, his dissertation project focuses on ontology-based resource discovery for research data in the humanities.
